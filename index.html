The Hexidecimal Color Model

In HTML and CSS, the hexidecimal color model is a way of representing colors using six values: #RRGGBB. #RRGGBB is the actual color, where R, G, and B are the actual colors - red, green, and blue, respectively.

The RGB Color Model

In HTML and CSS, the RGB color model is a way of representing colors using three values: Red, Green, and Blue. Red, Green, and Blue are the actual colors - red, green, and blue, respectively.

The RGB color model is an alternative to the HSL color model and is often more intuitive and easier to use. The RGB color model is also more accessible for users with color vision deficiencies.

The HSL Color Model

In HTML and CSS, the HSL color model is a way of representing colors using three values: Hue, Saturation, and Lightness. Hue is the actual color - red, blue, green, etc. Saturation is the vibrance of the color, with 0% being a shade of gray and 100% being the full color. Lightness is the brightness of the color, with 0% being black and 100% being white.

The HSL color model is an alternative to the RGB color model and is often more intuitive and easier to use. The HSL color model is also more accessible for users with color vision deficiencies.
